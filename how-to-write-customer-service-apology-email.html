Frequently Asked Questions

What's the ideal length for a professional email?

Five sentences or fewer is the gold standard for most business emails. If you genuinely need more detail, use bullet points to make the content scannable. The critical rule: your main request or question should be visible in the preview pane of any email client — that means it must appear in the first 2-3 lines. People scan emails on their phones during meetings; if your key message is buried in paragraph three, it probably won't be read at all.

How do I end a professional email effectively?

Your closing should match the tone and purpose of the email. For requests: 'Thanks in advance for your help with this.' For decisions: 'Please let me know your thoughts by Thursday.' For informational emails: 'No action needed — just keeping you in the loop.' Avoid generic closings like 'Best' when a specific, actionable closing would be more effective. The last line of your email sets the expectation for what happens next.

How can I make my emails sound more confident?

Remove hedging language like 'I just think,' 'Maybe we could,' 'Sort of,' and 'Sorry to bother you.' Replace with direct statements: 'I recommend,' 'Let's move forward with,' 'Here's the update.' Keep sentences shorter — confidence and brevity are closely linked. Read your draft aloud before sending; if you sound uncertain to yourself, you'll sound uncertain to your reader. Also, avoid overusing exclamation points and qualifiers that undermine your authority.
