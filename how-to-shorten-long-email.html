Frequently Asked Questions

How can I write emails that people actually read?

Put the most important information first — journalists call this the 'inverted pyramid.' Use short paragraphs (1-3 sentences max). Bold key dates, action items, and deadlines so they're visible even when scanning. If your email requires a response, ask for it explicitly and set a clear deadline. Avoid long background sections before getting to the point — busy recipients will stop reading before they reach your ask.

How do I maintain professionalism when I'm frustrated or angry?

Never send an email when you're actively upset. Write a draft if you need to process your thoughts, but save it and review it after at least 30 minutes — or better, the next morning. When you revise, strip out emotional language and focus on observable facts and specific impacts. If the matter is highly sensitive, consider whether email is even the right medium — sometimes a phone call or in-person conversation resolves tension faster and more effectively than a carefully worded message.

What's the ideal length for a professional email?

Five sentences or fewer is the gold standard for most business emails. If you genuinely need more detail, use bullet points to make the content scannable. The critical rule: your main request or question should be visible in the preview pane of any email client — that means it must appear in the first 2-3 lines. People scan emails on their phones during meetings; if your key message is buried in paragraph three, it probably won't be read at all.
