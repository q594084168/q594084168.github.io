Frequently Asked Questions

How do I give tough feedback to a high-performing employee?

Separate the person from the behavior. Start with genuine appreciation for their strengths, then address the specific behavior that needs to change, and end with confidence in their ability to improve. Be direct — high performers usually prefer candor over sugarcoating.

How do I announce bad news to my team without killing morale?

Be transparent about what happened and what you know. Share what you're doing about it and what support is available. Then give space for questions. Teams handle bad news better when leaders show vulnerability and a clear path forward.

How do I write a subject line that gets a response?

Be specific and action-oriented. Instead of 'Meeting,' write 'Decision needed: Q3 budget by Friday.' Instead of 'Hello,' write 'Quick question about the Johnson account.' The subject line should tell the recipient what you need and why they should open it.

What's the ideal length for a professional email?

Five sentences or fewer is the gold standard. If you need more, use bullet points. People scan emails on their phones — if your key message isn't visible in the preview pane, it probably won't be read.
