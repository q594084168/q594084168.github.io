Frequently Asked Questions

How do I write a subject line that gets a response?

Be specific and action-oriented. Instead of 'Meeting,' write 'Decision needed: Q3 budget by Friday.' Instead of 'Hello,' write 'Quick question about the Johnson account.' The subject line should tell the recipient what you need and why they should open it.

Should I use emojis in professional emails?

It depends on your workplace culture and relationship with the recipient. In formal external communication, avoid them. In internal Slack messages with close colleagues, a well-placed emoji can soften tone. When in doubt, leave them out.

What's the ideal length for a professional email?

Five sentences or fewer is the gold standard. If you need more, use bullet points. People scan emails on their phones — if your key message isn't visible in the preview pane, it probably won't be read.

How do I apologize for missing a meeting?

Acknowledge the impact, not just the fact. Say 'I apologize for missing our meeting and any inconvenience it caused' rather than just 'Sorry I missed it.' Offer to reschedule immediately and propose concrete times.
