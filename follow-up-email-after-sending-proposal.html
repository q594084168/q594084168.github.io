Frequently Asked Questions

What makes a cold email actually get opened?

A specific, curiosity-driven subject line that mentions something relevant to the recipient. Avoid 'Introduction' or 'Checking in.' Instead: 'Quick question about [their company's recent launch]' or 'Saw your post on [topic] — had a thought.' Personalization beats templates every time.

How can I make my emails sound more confident?

Remove hedging language like 'I just think,' 'Maybe we could,' and 'Sorry to bother you.' Replace with direct statements: 'I recommend,' 'Let's move forward with,' 'Here's the update.' Also, keep sentences shorter — confidence and brevity go hand in hand.

How do I write a subject line that gets a response?

Be specific and action-oriented. Instead of 'Meeting,' write 'Decision needed: Q3 budget by Friday.' Instead of 'Hello,' write 'Quick question about the Johnson account.' The subject line should tell the recipient what you need and why they should open it.

How many follow-ups is too many for a cold email?

Three is generally the limit before you risk annoying the recipient. Space them 3-7 days apart. If you get no response after three, move on — but consider testing a different subject line or value proposition with the next prospect.
