Frequently Asked Questions

What's different about writing emails for trades vs. office jobs?

Trades and hands-on professions value directness and reliability over corporate jargon. Skip the buzzwords. Focus on your certifications, safety record, and availability. A straightforward, honest tone builds more trust than polished corporate language.

How should I tailor my application email for a specific industry?

Research the industry's communication norms. Healthcare values clarity and compassion. Tech values conciseness and data. Creative fields allow more personality. Mirror the language used in the job description itself — it's the best clue to what the employer values.

How do I write a subject line that gets a response?

Be specific and action-oriented. Instead of 'Meeting,' write 'Decision needed: Q3 budget by Friday.' Instead of 'Hello,' write 'Quick question about the Johnson account.' The subject line should tell the recipient what you need and why they should open it.

How can I make my emails sound more confident?

Remove hedging language like 'I just think,' 'Maybe we could,' and 'Sorry to bother you.' Replace with direct statements: 'I recommend,' 'Let's move forward with,' 'Here's the update.' Also, keep sentences shorter — confidence and brevity go hand in hand.
