Frequently Asked Questions

How do I ask for a deposit upfront without scaring the client away?

Frame it as standard business practice, not a trust issue. Say 'To secure your spot in my schedule, I require a 50% deposit to begin work.' Present it as a policy, not a negotiation — confident freelancers get deposits.

What's the ideal length for a professional email?

Five sentences or fewer is the gold standard. If you need more, use bullet points. People scan emails on their phones — if your key message isn't visible in the preview pane, it probably won't be read.

How do I get testimonials from clients who are too busy?

Make it effortless. Draft a short testimonial yourself based on their previous feedback and ask 'Would you be comfortable with me using something like this?' Most clients will say yes or make minor tweaks — far easier than writing from scratch.

How do I write a subject line that gets a response?

Be specific and action-oriented. Instead of 'Meeting,' write 'Decision needed: Q3 budget by Friday.' Instead of 'Hello,' write 'Quick question about the Johnson account.' The subject line should tell the recipient what you need and why they should open it.
