Why How to Ask for a Raise Matters

Salary conversations are among the most stressful professional interactions. Research shows that people who negotiate their salary earn an average of $1 million more over their careers than those who don't. Yet most people avoid these conversations because they don't know how to phrase them professionally. The right words can make the difference between getting what you deserve and leaving money on the table.

How to Write a Great Salary Message

  1. 1Open with gratitude and enthusiasm for the role or your time at the company before transitioning to your request.
  2. 2Present market data and your specific contributions as evidence. Numbers like 'increased revenue by 30%' carry more weight than 'worked hard.'
  3. 3Use confident but collaborative language. 'Based on my research and contributions, I believe X is fair' works better than 'I demand X.'
  4. 4End with a forward-looking statement — express excitement about contributing more, not just about the money.

Before & After: Tone Transformation

Before — Unpolished

I think I deserve a raise because I've been working really hard and doing a good job. Can we talk about this?

After — Tone-Adjusted

I'd like to discuss my compensation. Over the past year, I've led the Q3 product launch that generated $1.2M in new revenue, reduced team churn by mentoring two junior developers, and consistently exceeded my OKRs. Based on market data for my role and contributions, I believe an adjustment to $X would better reflect my impact. I'm excited to continue driving results and would love to discuss this at your convenience.

Tips for Better Salary Communication

Timing matters more than most people realize. Schedule your raise conversation after a visible win — a successful project launch, a positive performance review, or a new budget cycle. Never ask during a crisis or when your manager is under pressure.

FAQ

What tone should I use when asking for a raise?

Be confident but not entitled. Frame your request around measurable value you've delivered — cite specific projects, revenue impact, or efficiency gains.

How do I time my raise request?

Schedule the conversation around positive momentum: after a successful project completion, a positive performance review, or at the start of a new budget cycle.

Should I negotiate salary via email?

Yes — email gives you time to craft your argument with market data. Express enthusiasm, present your counter-offer, and reaffirm excitement.
