Why Ask for Interview Feedback Matters

The interview process doesn't end when you walk out the door — your follow-up communication can tip the scales in your favor. Hiring managers consistently report that thoughtful follow-up emails signal professionalism and genuine interest. Yet most candidates either skip this step entirely or send generic templates that add no value.

FAQ

Will companies actually give feedback?

Some will, many won't due to legal concerns. It never hurts to ask politely.

How should I phrase the request?

Thank them for the opportunity, express continued interest in the company, and ask if they can share any areas for improvement.

What if the feedback is harsh?

View it as free career coaching. Even harsh feedback is valuable data for your next interview.
