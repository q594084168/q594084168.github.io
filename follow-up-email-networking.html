Why Follow Up After Networking Matters

Cold outreach is hard. Most of it ends up in spam folders or ignored. But personalized, well-crafted outreach that demonstrates genuine research and offers specific value still gets responses — and builds pipelines. The difference between a template blast and a tailored message isn't just response rate; it's the quality of relationships you build from the first email.

How to Write a Great Sales Message

  1. 1Research your recipient thoroughly before writing. Reference their company, role, or a recent achievement to show genuine interest.
  2. 2Lead with value, not your pitch. Open with an insight, observation, or question relevant to their world.
  3. 3Keep it short — under 100 words for cold outreach. The goal is to start a conversation, not close a deal.
  4. 4End with a clear, low-commitment ask. 'Would a 15-minute call work?' is better than 'Let me know if you're interested.'

I have 2-3 specific ideas for Acme's positioning that I think could be useful. Would a 15-minute call next week work?

Tips for Better Sales Communication

Personalization is the single biggest factor in cold email response rates. Emails with personalized subject lines get 50% higher open rates. Mention something specific about the recipient — a LinkedIn post, a company announcement, or a mutual connection.

FAQ

How soon after a networking event should I follow up?

Within 24 hours, ideally the next morning. The longer you wait, the more likely they forget who you are.

What should I reference from the conversation?

Something specific and memorable — a shared interest, a recommendation you made, or a question they asked. Specificity shows genuine attention.

Should I connect on LinkedIn too?

Yes — LinkedIn first, then email. A personalized connection request referencing your conversation is more effective than a generic one.
