Frequently Asked Questions

How can I make my emails sound more confident?

Remove hedging language like 'I just think,' 'Maybe we could,' and 'Sorry to bother you.' Replace with direct statements: 'I recommend,' 'Let's move forward with,' 'Here's the update.' Also, keep sentences shorter — confidence and brevity go hand in hand.

What's the ideal length for a professional email?

Five sentences or fewer is the gold standard. If you need more, use bullet points. People scan emails on their phones — if your key message isn't visible in the preview pane, it probably won't be read.

Is it OK to negotiate salary via email?

Yes, especially for remote positions. Email gives you time to craft your argument carefully. Start by expressing enthusiasm for the role, then present your counter-offer with market data to support it. End by reaffirming your excitement to join.

What tone should I use when asking for a raise?

Be confident but not entitled. Frame the request around the value you've delivered — use specific metrics if possible. Schedule a dedicated meeting rather than slipping it into a casual conversation. Practice your talking points beforehand.
