Frequently Asked Questions

What should I do if I haven't heard back after a final interview?

If a week has passed beyond the stated timeline, send a brief follow-up. Reference something specific from your conversation to show you were engaged, and ask if there's a revised timeline. If you still get no response after two attempts, it may be time to mentally move on — but keep the door open with a gracious final note.

Is it OK to negotiate salary via email?

Yes, especially for remote positions where email is the primary communication channel. Email actually gives you an advantage — it gives you time to craft your argument carefully with market data and specific examples of your value. Start by expressing genuine enthusiasm for the role, then present your counter-offer with supporting evidence, and end by reaffirming your excitement to join the team.

How do I ask for feedback after being rejected for a job?

Send a gracious email thanking them for the opportunity and for letting you know their decision. Politely ask if they'd be willing to share any feedback that could help you grow as a candidate. Make it clear you're not challenging their decision, just seeking constructive input. Some companies have policies against giving feedback, so don't take silence personally.

How can I make my emails sound more confident?

Remove hedging language like 'I just think,' 'Maybe we could,' 'Sort of,' and 'Sorry to bother you.' Replace with direct statements: 'I recommend,' 'Let's move forward with,' 'Here's the update.' Keep sentences shorter — confidence and brevity are closely linked. Read your draft aloud before sending; if you sound uncertain to yourself, you'll sound uncertain to your reader. Also, avoid overusing exclamation points and qualifiers that undermine your authority.
