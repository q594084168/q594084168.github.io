Frequently Asked Questions

How do I deal with a micromanaging boss?

Proactively communicate. Send brief daily or weekly updates before they ask. This builds trust and often reduces their anxiety-driven need to check in. If the behavior persists, request a conversation about autonomy and how you work best.

How do I write a subject line that gets a response?

Be specific and action-oriented. Instead of 'Meeting,' write 'Decision needed: Q3 budget by Friday.' Instead of 'Hello,' write 'Quick question about the Johnson account.' The subject line should tell the recipient what you need and why they should open it.

What's the ideal length for a professional email?

Five sentences or fewer is the gold standard. If you need more, use bullet points. People scan emails on their phones — if your key message isn't visible in the preview pane, it probably won't be read.

How do I push back on an unrealistic deadline?

Don't just say no — present alternatives. Outline what can realistically be delivered by the deadline, what would require more time, and what tradeoffs exist. Ask 'Which of these priorities is most important?' to shift the conversation from yes/no to collaborative planning.
