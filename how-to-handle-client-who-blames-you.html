Frequently Asked Questions

What's the ideal length for a professional email?

Five sentences or fewer is the gold standard. If you need more, use bullet points. People scan emails on their phones — if your key message isn't visible in the preview pane, it probably won't be read.

How can I make my emails sound more confident?

Remove hedging language like 'I just think,' 'Maybe we could,' and 'Sorry to bother you.' Replace with direct statements: 'I recommend,' 'Let's move forward with,' 'Here's the update.' Also, keep sentences shorter — confidence and brevity go hand in hand.

How do I tell a client they are wrong without offending them?

Lead with curiosity, not accusation. Say 'I may be looking at this differently — could we walk through the data together?' This positions you as a collaborative problem-solver rather than a critic.

When should I stop chasing an unpaid invoice and take legal action?

After 90 days and at least three documented reminders, consider your options. Before legal action, send a final 'notice of intent' email stating clearly what will happen if payment isn't received by a specific date. Often this alone prompts action.
