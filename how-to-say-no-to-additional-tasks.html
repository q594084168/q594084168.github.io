Frequently Asked Questions

How long should I wait before following up after an interview?

Send a thank-you email within 24 hours of your interview — this is non-negotiable. If the interviewer gave you a timeline for decisions and it has passed, wait an additional 3-5 business days before sending a polite follow-up. Keep your tone gracious and understanding: acknowledge they're busy, reaffirm your enthusiasm, and ask if there's any additional information you can provide.

What should I do if I haven't heard back after a final interview?

If a week has passed beyond the stated timeline, send a brief follow-up. Reference something specific from your conversation to show you were engaged, and ask if there's a revised timeline. If you still get no response after two attempts, it may be time to mentally move on — but keep the door open with a gracious final note.

How do I write a subject line that actually gets a response?

Be specific and action-oriented. Instead of 'Meeting,' write 'Decision needed: Q3 budget proposal by Friday.' Instead of 'Hello,' write 'Quick question about the Johnson account renewal.' The subject line should immediately tell the recipient: (1) what this is about, (2) what action is needed from them, and (3) when. Aim for under 50 characters for mobile readability.

How do I write a resignation email that keeps doors open?

Keep your resignation email brief, positive, and professional. State your intention to resign, provide your last working day (standard is two weeks' notice), express genuine gratitude for the opportunities you've had, and offer to help with the transition. Avoid detailed explanations of why you're leaving — that conversation belongs in person, not in writing. The goal is to leave a lasting impression of professionalism.
