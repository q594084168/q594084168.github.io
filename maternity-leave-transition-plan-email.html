Frequently Asked Questions

How do I get testimonials from clients who are too busy?

Make it effortless for them. Draft a short testimonial yourself based on their previous positive feedback and ask: 'Would you be comfortable with me using something like this?' Most clients will say yes or make minor tweaks. This approach is far more effective than asking them to write something from scratch — you've removed the friction while still getting their authentic endorsement.

How do I ask for a deposit upfront without scaring the client away?

Frame it as standard business practice, not a personal trust issue. Say 'To secure your project slot, our standard process requires a 50% deposit to begin work.' Present it as a policy that applies to all clients, not a special condition for them. Confident freelancers who treat deposits as routine get them with minimal pushback. If a client refuses a reasonable deposit, that's a red flag about their willingness to pay at all.

When should I raise my freelance rates?

Announce rate increases at natural business milestones: the new year, after completing a major project successfully, or when you've measurably upgraded your skills. Give existing clients 30-60 days' notice before the increase takes effect. Consider grandfathering your longest-standing, most reliable clients at the old rate — their stability and loyalty have value beyond the incremental revenue.

How do I write a subject line that actually gets a response?

Be specific and action-oriented. Instead of 'Meeting,' write 'Decision needed: Q3 budget proposal by Friday.' Instead of 'Hello,' write 'Quick question about the Johnson account renewal.' The subject line should immediately tell the recipient: (1) what this is about, (2) what action is needed from them, and (3) when. Aim for under 50 characters for mobile readability.
