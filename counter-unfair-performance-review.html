Why Counter Unfair Performance Review Matters

Day-to-day workplace communication shapes your professional reputation more than any single presentation or project. Calling in sick, resigning, pushing back on workload — these routine interactions are micro-opportunities to build trust and demonstrate professionalism. Get them right, and you're seen as reliable and mature. Get them wrong, and you create friction that compounds over time.

FAQ

Should I dispute a review in writing?

Yes — written rebuttals create a paper trail. Be factual, not emotional. Reference specific examples and data.

What evidence should I include?

Emails from colleagues praising your work, project metrics, completed goals, and any prior positive feedback that contradicts the review.

What if they don't change the review?

Request that your written rebuttal be attached to the review in your personnel file. Then focus on exceeding expectations going forward.
