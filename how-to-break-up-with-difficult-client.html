Frequently Asked Questions

How do I write a subject line that gets a response?

Be specific and action-oriented. Instead of 'Meeting,' write 'Decision needed: Q3 budget by Friday.' Instead of 'Hello,' write 'Quick question about the Johnson account.' The subject line should tell the recipient what you need and why they should open it.

What if a client keeps making excuses for late payment?

After the second missed deadline, escalate politely but firmly. Set a specific final date and mention that you'll need to pause work until the balance is cleared. Document all communication. Most clients respect clear boundaries.

What's the ideal length for a professional email?

Five sentences or fewer is the gold standard. If you need more, use bullet points. People scan emails on their phones — if your key message isn't visible in the preview pane, it probably won't be read.

How do I ask a client for payment without damaging the relationship?

Frame your reminder as helpful rather than accusatory. Start with 'I wanted to make sure the invoice didn't get lost in your inbox' rather than 'You haven't paid yet.' Attach the original invoice for convenience and offer to discuss payment terms if needed.
