Frequently Asked Questions

How do I get testimonials from clients who are too busy?

Make it effortless. Draft a short testimonial yourself based on their previous feedback and ask 'Would you be comfortable with me using something like this?' Most clients will say yes or make minor tweaks — far easier than writing from scratch.

When should I raise my freelance rates?

Announce rate increases at natural milestones: the new year, after completing a major project, or when your skills have measurably improved. Give existing clients 30-60 days notice and consider grandfathering loyal clients at the old rate for a transition period.

How can I make my emails sound more confident?

Remove hedging language like 'I just think,' 'Maybe we could,' and 'Sorry to bother you.' Replace with direct statements: 'I recommend,' 'Let's move forward with,' 'Here's the update.' Also, keep sentences shorter — confidence and brevity go hand in hand.

How do I ask for a deposit upfront without scaring the client away?

Frame it as standard business practice, not a trust issue. Say 'To secure your spot in my schedule, I require a 50% deposit to begin work.' Present it as a policy, not a negotiation — confident freelancers get deposits.
