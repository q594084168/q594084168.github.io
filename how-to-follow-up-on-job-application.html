Frequently Asked Questions

How do I follow up after a job application without being annoying?

Wait 5-7 business days after submitting your application. Keep your follow-up brief: reference the position title, reiterate your interest, and ask if there's anything else you can provide. Avoid phrases like 'just checking in' — they add no value.

How can I make my emails sound more confident?

Remove hedging language like 'I just think,' 'Maybe we could,' and 'Sorry to bother you.' Replace with direct statements: 'I recommend,' 'Let's move forward with,' 'Here's the update.' Also, keep sentences shorter — confidence and brevity go hand in hand.

How do I write a subject line that gets a response?

Be specific and action-oriented. Instead of 'Meeting,' write 'Decision needed: Q3 budget by Friday.' Instead of 'Hello,' write 'Quick question about the Johnson account.' The subject line should tell the recipient what you need and why they should open it.

How long should I wait before following up after an interview?

Send a thank-you email within 24 hours. If you haven't heard back and the timeline they gave has passed, wait an additional 3-5 business days before following up. Keep it gracious, not pushy.
