Frequently Asked Questions

How do I handle scope creep from a client?

Address it the first time it happens, not after it's become a pattern. Say 'That's a great idea — it's outside our original scope, so would you like me to put together a separate estimate for this additional work?' This acknowledges their idea positively while establishing that additional work requires additional budget. Document all scope agreements in writing to prevent future misunderstandings.

How do I get testimonials from clients who are too busy?

Make it effortless for them. Draft a short testimonial yourself based on their previous positive feedback and ask: 'Would you be comfortable with me using something like this?' Most clients will say yes or make minor tweaks. This approach is far more effective than asking them to write something from scratch — you've removed the friction while still getting their authentic endorsement.

When should I raise my freelance rates?

Announce rate increases at natural business milestones: the new year, after completing a major project successfully, or when you've measurably upgraded your skills. Give existing clients 30-60 days' notice before the increase takes effect. Consider grandfathering your longest-standing, most reliable clients at the old rate — their stability and loyalty have value beyond the incremental revenue.

How can I write emails that people actually read?

Put the most important information first — journalists call this the 'inverted pyramid.' Use short paragraphs (1-3 sentences max). Bold key dates, action items, and deadlines so they're visible even when scanning. If your email requires a response, ask for it explicitly and set a clear deadline. Avoid long background sections before getting to the point — busy recipients will stop reading before they reach your ask.
