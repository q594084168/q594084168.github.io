Frequently Asked Questions

How do I disagree with my boss's decision?

Frame your disagreement as an attempt to help achieve the best outcome, not as a challenge to their authority. Use data and specific examples rather than opinions. Say 'I see it differently — could I share my perspective?' rather than 'I think you're wrong.' Propose alternatives, not just criticism. And choose your battles wisely — not every disagreement is worth pursuing.

How do I write a subject line that actually gets a response?

Be specific and action-oriented. Instead of 'Meeting,' write 'Decision needed: Q3 budget proposal by Friday.' Instead of 'Hello,' write 'Quick question about the Johnson account renewal.' The subject line should immediately tell the recipient: (1) what this is about, (2) what action is needed from them, and (3) when. Aim for under 50 characters for mobile readability.

How do I say 'that's not my job' professionally?

Don't use those exact words. Instead, redirect constructively: 'I believe that falls under [specific department or role]. I'd recommend checking with [name] who handles that area — they'd have the most up-to-date information.' If it's something you could do but shouldn't be responsible for, add: 'I'm happy to help this time, but going forward, could we clarify whose scope this falls under?'

How do I push back on an unrealistic deadline?

Don't just say no — present alternatives based on data. Break down what can realistically be delivered by the deadline, what would require more time, and what tradeoffs exist between scope, quality, and timeline. Ask 'Which of these priorities is most important right now?' to shift the conversation from a yes/no confrontation to collaborative problem-solving. Your manager wants solutions, not just problems.
