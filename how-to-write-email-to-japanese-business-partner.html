Frequently Asked Questions

How do I write a subject line that gets a response?

Be specific and action-oriented. Instead of 'Meeting,' write 'Decision needed: Q3 budget by Friday.' Instead of 'Hello,' write 'Quick question about the Johnson account.' The subject line should tell the recipient what you need and why they should open it.

What's the ideal length for a professional email?

Five sentences or fewer is the gold standard. If you need more, use bullet points. People scan emails on their phones — if your key message isn't visible in the preview pane, it probably won't be read.

What if English isn't my first language — how do I make sure my email sounds natural?

Keep sentences short. Avoid idioms that might not translate. Use tools like this one to check your tone! Most importantly, don't apologize for your English — your ideas matter more than perfect grammar. Many native speakers are worse communicators than skilled non-native speakers.

How do cultural differences affect business email tone?

American business culture tends toward directness and informality. European cultures (especially German and Nordic) value precision and structure. Asian business cultures often prefer indirectness, relationship-building, and formal titles. Research the specific country's norms before hitting send.
